Mental Health Disorders Around the World

Mental health involves aspects including psychological, emotional, and social well-being. It plays a large role in how we feel, think, and behave. 2.2 million Australians have reported having a mental health condition, according to the 2022 Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS) data.

Prevalence of Anxiety disorders around the world 2019

Individuals suffer from anxiety disorders worldwide. The countries with the most prevalent cases of people with anxiety disorder are Portugal, New Zealand, Brazil, Ireland and Switzerland.

Comorbidities between disorders

Mental health disorders are often comorbid, meaning that there is usually a two or more mental health conditins that individuals suffer from simultaneously. There is a positive relationship between the prevalence of alcohol use and drug use.

Mental Health in Australia

Prevalence of Mental Disorders in Australia

Does the likelihood of developing a mental health disorder change as we age? The ABS defines any long-term mental disorder as any mental disorder that has been occurring for at least 12 months. Evidently, prevalence of mental disorders decrease as age increases. It can also be seen that females have a higher prevalence of mental health disorders than men.

Strategies Used for Mental Health

Individuals cope with their mental health in a variety of ways. In Australia, out of those with mental health disorders, the method used with the highest prevalence is through increasing the level of exercise/physical activity undertaken, followed by practising positive thinking. Cutting alcohol or drugs is the least prevalent method of dealing with mental health issues.
